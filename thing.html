CAMEL
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • In Arabian culture, they are so iconic that there are more than 160 words which mean ‘camel’.
  • Ancient Greeks, Romans, and Persians used to ride camels while fighting battles.
CHAMELEON
  • The two largest species of chameleon on record, the Oustalet's and the Parson's chameleon can grow to 27 inches
  • Contrary to popular belief, chameleons mainly change colour to communicate or regulate body temperature
  • Their eyes have an incredible range of motion and can move in completely opposite directions.
TEDDY BEAR
  • The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a hunting trip.
  • Bears can walk on their hind legs, so some Native Americans call them "the beasts that walk like man."
  • Bears are one of the most intelligent land animals in North America.
PANDA
  • In China, pandas are known as ‘da xiong mao’, which means giant bear cat.
  • 99% of a Giant Panda’s diet is made up of the bamboo plant.
  • They spend up to sixteen hours a day eating to gain enough sustenance.
PIGEON
  • Pigeons are very clever. They recognise themselves in mirrors, something few animals can do
  • Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.
  • Pigeons can find their way back to their nests from 1300 miles away.
CICADA
  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • cicadas' shells are used in traditional chinese medicine.
  • Desert cicadas are one of the few insects which cool themselves by sweating
